BY

The first BMW M3

The BMW E30 is the popular, second-generation 3 Series, produced from 1982 to 1994, known for its balanced design, engaging driving dynamics, and strong engineering, which includes four-door saloons, coupes, convertibles, and wagons, and is considered a classic that helped define the "ultimate driving machine" reputation for BMW.

The E30 is widely regarded as a classic car and helped solidify the 3 Series' reputation as a stylish, practical, and fun compact sports saloon. Its powerful engines and handling capabilities were also key to BMW's success in various forms of motor sport, including DTM and rallying. The driver-angled dashboard and short-throw, firm gear shift emphasize the driver-focused nature of the E30.

All E30s came with front disc brakes and the same basic suspension setup, but the 316 and 318 entry level cars all had fairly lethargic ‘M10’ four-cylinder engines with a single overhead cam. The engines’ roots could be traced back to the early 1960s, and you could feel it. It was the six-cylinder cars that impressed the most at launch, with 2.0 and 2.3-litre ‘M20’ engines that felt much more alive and gave the small BMW some proper sporty credentials.
